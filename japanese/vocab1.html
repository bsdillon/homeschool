Remaining Kana

I know. You thought we were done with this, but there are just a few more things to know. First, some of the kana have diacritics. These are markings on the hiragana or katakana that give them a voiced sound. All the ones you have learned thus are are unvoiced. Put your hand on your cheek and say "there". Now say "tare". These words are the same but one is voiced and one is unvoiced. You can feel the vibrations in your voice box on "there". So you will find that the kana for K become G, S becomes Z/J, T becomes D, and H becomes a B/F. Take a look.

Kがぎぐげごガギグゲゴ
Z/Jざじずぜぞザジズゼゾ
Dだぢづでどダヂヅデド
Bばびぶべぼバビブベボ
Pぱぴぷぺぽパピプペポ

There are two more kana symbols that we need to know. These are ー and を/ヲ. Some words have looong sounds in them which people write as "Dōbutsu" with an overline. In the kana we write that as "どーぶつ" showing that the ど is held for two beats. The last kana is "WO" or を/ヲ. It is used in the grammar and should be recognized. This is not a big deal and you will learn it quickly now that we are working on sentences.
