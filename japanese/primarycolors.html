Learn Colors

Many colors in English came from things that people saw in nature. Once upon a time there was no color "blue". Then someone found beautiful blue rocks called lapis lazuli arrived in the mediterranean from Afghanistan. Some cultures took the name "azul" for that color. In the north, the Vikings used the name of a small berry--the blaeberry--with a similar bold blue. Both versions spread into Europe. Many colors in Japanese have origins. Oranges, yolks, ashes, peaches, and tea. Anything + 'いろ' is a color.
